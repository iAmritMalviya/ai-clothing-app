## Winning Strategy

### Core Thesis

**Scalio wins on features. We win on distribution.**

Scalio is a web app. We are a Telegram bot. This isn't a small difference — it's a fundamentally different go-to-market.

A retailer in Surat Textile Market doesn't open a browser, sign up, choose a plan, navigate a dashboard, and upload photos. He opens Telegram, sends a photo, and gets a catalog image back. **The product IS the distribution channel.**

### Strategy: 3 Phases

---

### Phase 1: Fix Quality, Lock Distribution (Weeks 1-3)

**Goal**: Image quality good enough that retailers share results on WhatsApp/Instagram.

If quality is bad, nothing else matters. A retailer who gets one bad result never returns.

**Actions:**
1. Switch to `gemini-3.1-flash-image-preview` (5K free images/month, 2K resolution, thinkingConfig)
2. Get FASHN API key → use Model Create for 4 high-quality Indian male presets
3. Test FASHN Product-to-Model (skip presets entirely — flat-lay → on-model in one call)
4. Add narrative prompts (3.2x quality improvement per Google's data)
5. Add `imageSize: '2K'` + `aspectRatio: '3:4'` (Myntra-standard ratio)
6. Test with 20 real garment photos from local market
7. **Quality gate**: If a retailer wouldn't share the result on their Instagram, it's not good enough.

**Metrics:**
- [ ] 8/10 test images rated "good enough for Instagram" by 3 real retailers
- [ ] Processing time < 30 seconds
- [ ] Zero headless/distorted outputs

---

### Phase 2: Seed Users from Cloth Markets (Weeks 3-6)

**Goal**: 50 approved users, 10 active daily users.

**Distribution playbook:**
1. **Surat Textile Market visit** (physical)
   - Walk through Ring Road market with phone
   - Demo ModelWala to shop owners: "Send me a photo of your best-selling kurta"
   - Show result in 15 seconds on their phone
   - If impressed, show them the bot: "This is free for now. Send photos, get catalog."
   - Target: 20 shops in one visit

2. **WhatsApp group seeding**
   - Join Surat/Tirupur/Ludhiana textile trading groups
   - Share before/after: flat-lay photo → ModelWala result
   - "Free AI catalog generator for clothing sellers. Message @ModelWalaBot on Telegram"
   - Don't spam. Post once, let results speak.

3. **Instagram content**
   - Post 3 before/after reels per week
   - "This Rs 200 kurta looks like it belongs on Myntra"
   - Tag #SuratTextile #ClothBusiness #FashionSeller
   - DM sellers who engage → invite to bot

4. **Referral loop**
   - Approved users can refer others
   - "Send /invite in the bot → get a referral link"
   - Referred user gets priority approval

**Metrics:**
- [ ] 50 approved users
- [ ] 10 generating daily
- [ ] 5 sharing results on Instagram/WhatsApp organically

## The Moat We're Building

```
Week 1-3:  Quality (can't grow without it)
           ↓
Week 3-6:  Distribution (Telegram, cloth market visits, WhatsApp groups)
           ↓
Week 6-10: Network effects (retailers share results → new retailers join)
           ↓
Week 10+:  Habit (daily use → can't switch → paying customer)
           ↓
Long-term: Data (garment photos → train custom models → quality moat)
```

**The compounding advantage:**
1. Every garment photo processed = training data for better models
2. Every satisfied user = word-of-mouth to 10 other retailers
3. Every cloth market visit = locked-in community
4. Telegram habit = switching cost (they won't download Scalio's app)

**Scalio can copy our features. They can't copy our distribution.**

A web app company building a Telegram bot is an afterthought. A Telegram-native product built for Indian retailers is the core identity. This is why we win.
